Research Experience

  • 2025 – 2026
    Closed-Loop Active Learning for Sparse Neural Encoding in Macaque AIT Cortex
    Peking University — supervised by Prof. Shiming Tang
    • Constructed a virtual neural response testbed based on the Maximum Entropy Principle to simulate sparse and specific encoding characteristics of the Macaque AIT cortex.
    • Developed a GPU-accelerated closed-loop active learning system utilizing novel algorithms to overcome the "sampling miss" bottleneck for extremely sparse neurons.
    • Formulated the closed-loop sampling process as a Markov Decision Process (MDP) and applied Reinforcement Learning (DQN and PPO) to dynamically optimize retrieval of rare, high-response features.
  • 2025 – 2026
    Brain-Inspired Representation Learning via ResNet-JEPA
    Peking University — supervised by Prof. Shiming Tang
    • Redesigned the standard CNN-JEPA framework to construct a novel ResNet-JEPA model, inspired by the predictive coding theory of the brain.
    • Framed self-supervised visual pre-training as a biological predictive process, mimicking how the primate visual cortex continuously anticipates incoming sensory inputs in latent spaces.
    • Investigated the alignment between the model's latent-space predictive dynamics and hierarchical representational mechanisms in the ventral visual stream.
  • 2024 – 2025
    Improvement of Equivariant Graph Neural Networks for 3D Atomistic Systems
    Peking University — supervised by Prof. Muhan Zhang
    • Improved the EGNN model based on SOTA model GotenNet, developing the pro-EGNN model.
    • Combined scalarization-based models and high-degree steerable models, maintaining the ability to encode high-degree geometric features while achieving computational efficiency.
    • pro-EGNN outperforms the original EGNN on the QM9 dataset for 3D atomistic systems.
  • 2024 – 2025
    RNA Editing and RNA Biology
    Peking University — supervised by Prof. Chengqi Yi (Yilab)
    • Participated in improving the RESTART RNA editing system and exploring suppressor tRNA biology.
    • Engineered tRNA with natural and artificial modifications.
  • 2022 – 2023
    Function and Regulation of the TCP Gene Family in Arabidopsis and Soybean
    Peking University — supervised by Prof. Genji Qin
    • Investigated the regulatory roles of TCP gene family by constructing multiple mutants in Arabidopsis and soybean.
    • Focused on the roles of TCP genes in leaf development process.
